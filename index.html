I am a PhD student at the Computing Sciences Department, University of Alberta, Canada, advised by Prof. Michael Bowling.
My research work focuses on how AI systems learn if feedback is not always observable. How do design agents learn to avoid causing harm to humans or other agents or being destructive to the environment or themselves without human supervision
or prior knowledge?
Before joining PhD, I was an AI engineer at SonyAI for three years, working on designing a multi-agent high-dynamic environment in a physics simulator, training robust agents cooperatively and competitively
with self-play and goal-conditioned RL, transferring learned policies to the real world, and integrating them with vision systems and robot control methods.
